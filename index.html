Day 1 Week 1 — Catching the loop
Thought labelling
Before you can change anything, you need to notice it happening. Today is only about awareness — not fixing, not stopping. Just noticing. Two words do all the work: "There it is."
Concept
What it is
You name what the thought is doing — not what it's saying. Replaying. Scripting. Decoding. One word creates distance.
Practice
The "there it is" method
Catch the thought. Say "there it is. [label]." Return to something physical. Repeat without frustration.
Awareness cue for today
The moment you catch yourself replaying a conversation that already happened — say internally: "There it is. Replaying." That's it. No need to stop it. Just name it.
Your labels for today
ReplayingScripting DecodingPerforming Checking closenessLooping
Science behind it
Naming a mental state reduces its emotional intensity (Lieberman, UCLA 2007). Affect labelling activates the prefrontal cortex and reduces amygdala reactivity — literally calming the alarm by naming it.

The 5 deeper themes
Fear of abandonment & clinging
Problems 5, 6, 13
Love and closeness feel like they must be continuously earned. The underlying belief: if I stop showing up, I disappear from their life.
Need for validation & being seen
Problems 7, 9, 13
A quiet hunger to matter — to be recognised, chosen, remembered. Not ego. The longing of someone who hasn't always felt sufficiently visible.
Emotional sensitivity & mood vulnerability
Problems 1, 4, 11
A highly attuned nervous system. Small things carry large emotional weight. This is the cost and the gift of deep feeling.
Avoidance as self-protection
Problems 3, 9, 10
Retreating before being rejected — pulling away from connection, attention, or love before it can be taken away.
Mind as both ally and adversary
Problems 2, 8, 12
A powerful analytical mind turned inward without rest. The same intelligence that helps you understand deeply also refuses to let simple things simply be.

For: Mood instability
All 13 mantras — one for each pattern
Mood instability
"This feeling will pass. It is not who I am — it is just a wave."
Use when: a small thing has suddenly shifted your mood and you feel guilty about it.
Overthinking
"Is this analysis helping me — or haunting me? Enough for now. Let it breathe."
Use when: you've been turning the same thought over for the third time with no new conclusion.
Isolation & withdrawal
"I don't have to explain everything. But I can send one message: I'm not okay right now, but I'll be back."
Use when: you feel the pull to go completely silent and disappear.
Guilt after disappointing others
"One moment doesn't define me. If it needs correction, I'll do it gently — not by erasing myself."
Use when: you're replaying something you said and spiralling into guilt.
Compulsive message checking
"Silence is not rejection. My pace is not a problem. Responsiveness is kind — but boundaries are sacred."
Use when: you feel the urge to check or reply even though you don't want to.
Clinging to relationships
"Before messaging, ask: am I doing this from connection — or from fear?"
Use when: you're about to send a message driven by anxiety about losing someone.
Self-glorifying inner narratives
"I am not here to impress the world. I am here to express myself honestly."
Use when: you catch yourself in a mental movie where everyone finally sees how good you are.
Scenario building
"Let life unfold — I don't always have to script it. Scenarios are weather passing through the sky of my mind."
Use when: you're deep in an imagined future conversation or confrontation.
Craving attention but rejecting it
"I can enjoy being seen without becoming dependent. I am worthy of presence."
Use when: someone shows you care and you feel the urge to push it away.
Conflicted about relationships
"I am lovable even in my waiting. Love will find me living — not chasing."
Use when: longing for connection is tipping into frustration or forcing.
Comfort in sadness
"Sadness is a visitor, not a home — even if I've lived in it for years."
Use when: sadness feels safe and familiar and you notice yourself settling into it.
Constant overanalysis
"Not everything is a riddle. Some moments want to be felt, not figured out."
Use when: you're dissecting an experience instead of just being in it.
Hurt when not first to know
"Their timing is not always a measure of my worth. Importance is not always measured in 'first to know'."
Use when: a friend told someone else something before telling you.
